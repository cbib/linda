Linda use different ontologies to help scientists describing their experimental design (treatments, growing conditions, etc.) and their environmental conditions; An ontology is a way of showing the properties of a subject area and how they are related, by defining a set of concepts and categories that represent the subject.

Ontologies available in Linda

Plant Environment Ontology (EO)

A structured, controlled vocabulary which describes the treatments, growing conditions, and/or study types used in plant biology experiments.

Ressources link: EO

Plant Environment Ontology (PO)

The Plant Ontology is a structured vocabulary and database resource that links plant anatomy, morphology and growth and development to plant genomics data.

Ressources link: PO

XEML Environment Ontology (XEO)

XeO has been created to help plant scientists in documenting and sharing metadata describing the abiotic environment.

Ressources link: XeO

Environment Ontology (EnvO)

ENVO is an ontology which represents knowledge about environments,environmental processes, ecosystems, habitats, and related entities. It interoperates with other ontologies in the OBO Foundry and Library. New terms or revisions can be requested at https://github.com/EnvironmentOntology/envo/issues/ Please see www.environmentontology.org for more information and citations.

Ressources link: Environment Ontology (EnvO)
